books

i like to read books. a lot, actually. although these days i don't have as much time to read as i would like.

here are some that i have read in 2025 (mostly the summer) that i liked.

  • A Little Life by Hanya Yanagihara -- this one was intense. i had to stop in the middle twice. it made me feel a lot of things though and for that i enjoyed it.
  • In the Dream House by Carmen Maria Machado -- fantastic. she is so good at the deconstruction and reconstruction and the chopping up of stories while keeping you still hooked.
  • Kafka on the Shore by Haruki Murakami -- my favorite murakami book so far. i love how detached he is about writing about our little mundane lives and how he can still make them feel as infinite as they feel. i need to get this one back from my friend who i lent it to.
  • Dance Dance Dance by Haruki Murakami -- i liked this one more than the first by a lot. cool dolphin hotel and cool teenage girl yuki and cool rock music. mundane man supernatural encounters.
  • Norwegian Wood by Haruki Murakami -- at this point it is evident i went on a murakami rampage. this was great. i liked it a lot. i like the simplicity of it all, but how it feels so big. i think i read it in a sitting on the staten island ferry back and forth and back and forth.
  • A Wild Sheep Chase by Haruki Murakami -- there is a page of this one that i adore. about the summer. and the mundanity of it all. the rest wasn't as good as dance dance dance.

here are some that i have read in 2025 that i liked less.

  • The Hole by Hiroko Oyamada -- a little confusing and disturbing. short read. beautiful cover though.
  • Sputnik Sweetheart by Haruki Murakami -- not quite as colorful as the others.

here are some that i started in 2025 but have yet to finish.

  • East of Eden by Steinbeck -- i will try.
